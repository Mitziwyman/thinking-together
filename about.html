About Mitzi Wyman

My work sits at the intersection of two things that are usually kept separate: the inner life of leadership — the clarity, courage and wisdom a leader needs to bring to genuinely difficult situations — and the organisation itself, where it is going, what it is investing in, whether its thinking is expanding or just rehearsing what it already knows.

The Thinking Environment is where those two things meet. When people are given genuine attention, something shifts. Leaders find access to their own best thinking. Teams stop performing consensus and start thinking together. I've worked with organisations across sectors — NHS trusts, Sony Pictures, the Judicial College, Cabinet Office, and others — where the common thread is not the industry but the complexity of the leadership challenge.

"I came away replenished with food for thought. It was a new experience for me and a privilege to be in conversation with so many sharp minds from a notably diverse range of backgrounds."
Sir Roderic Lyne, Vice-Chairman, Chatham House

How I work

I work with organisations over time. The work usually begins with a question the team can't quite answer, or a sense that the conversations in the room aren't reaching what matters most.

What I bring isn't just facilitation. Alongside the thinking work, I build digital tools — diagnostics and companions — that stay with the organisation after I do. The Meeting Intelligence Index shows what's actually happening in a team's meetings. Companion tools keep the thinking alive between sessions. The work doesn't end when I leave the room.

At a moment when most organisations are uncertain about AI, I've been working at that intersection — building intelligent tools, speaking and writing about what it means for how we lead, and helping leaders stay ahead of it rather than behind it.

By creating a thinking environment that is inclusive and promotes fresh, clear thinking, the meetings became more productive and engaging. Applying this in practice was a joy with immediate results."

Background

My career began in law — environmental and social justice — which is where my values took root. I've served as a non-executive director on an NHS board and as a senior consultant with the King's Fund. As a Senior Advisor to the International Integrated Reporting Council, I worked with the Six Capitals framework long before it entered leadership development — that's where the systems thinking in my practice comes from.

I'm a member of the Time to Think Faculty, trained directly by Nancy Kline, and I facilitate residential programmes for Windsor Leadership Trust. I founded Fulcrum Leaders in 2011 — a network for leaders thinking across boundaries — which shaped much of how I think about dialogue and systems.
