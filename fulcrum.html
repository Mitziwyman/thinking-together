Fulcrum

A cross-sector forum for leaders, convened to create the kind of dialogue most senior people rarely get access to.

Provenance

A space for rare, open dialogue

In 2011, with the support of Jim Clifford OBE, I founded Fulcrum — a forum bringing together leaders from health, law, the arts, business and public service. For over a decade, Fulcrum's salons and colloquia created a space where people could step outside the maelstrom of public life and think for themselves: on authority, on betrayal and forgiveness, on sustainability, on intergenerational equity, and on the questions that rarely get airtime in the rooms most leaders normally occupy.

The approach was simple: listen to each other, speak with thoughtfulness and kindness, and encourage each other in believing that the challenges and injustices we see can be addressed. Fulcrum's gatherings were generously sponsored by KPMG, Arup and Mazars, and brought together guests from across sectors — including, on occasion, the Vice-Chair of Chatham House and members of St Ethelburga's Centre for Reconciliation and Peace.

Fulcrum's spirit continues in my current work, and its next chapter is taking shape.

Reflections from members

"Usually when I meet with other CEOs and leaders, it is to discuss common topics, current issues or forthcoming challenges. I rarely have the opportunity to share insights with people from a wide range of backgrounds who are not trying to collectively 'problem-solve' and are instead focused on deeply listening to each other. It is what Wordsworth called 'emotion recollected in tranquility', and it brings out the poetry in all of us."
Alistair MaCapra, CE, CIPR
"I came away replenished with food for thought. I was intrigued to be invited to the Fulcrum Dialogue. It was a new experience for me and a privilege to be in conversation with so many sharp minds from a notably diverse range of backgrounds."
Sir Roderic Lyne, V-C, Chatham House
"It was a fantastic experience — a safe space to be open and consider a range of views about some fundamental issues impacting on people's lives today, both work-based and personal. The dialogic approach provides a new way to do business. The public and private sectors alike should learn from this approach as it could change the dynamics of business and motivate people."
Gillian Fawcett, former Head of Global, Cipfa
