Thinking in Time —
a horizons companion

You've worked with the Three Horizons — a way of seeing where a system is now, where it's heading, and the space in between where the new is finding its feet. This companion helps you carry that lens into your week. A few minutes, once a week: where has your thinking been living, and what is that making possible?

✎

Best with a notebook alongside it. The questions are prompts; the thinking is yours. Writing something down, unhurried, is itself a way of stepping outside the rush.

The horizons, briefly

H1 — keeping the system working.

Delivery, reporting, the programme as commissioned — necessary work that holds the memory and the machinery.

H2 — the new finding its feet.

Pilots and partnerships proving their value in a system that hasn't yet made room for them — the contested middle where innovation work lives.

H3 — the future asking questions.

Work that has no home yet: what health in this region could become.

One

Where your best attention went

Think of one piece of work this week that had your best attention. Which horizon was it serving?

There's no right answer — Horizon 1 work done with full attention is leadership. Notice too whether the thinking matched the question: the trouble is rarely too much time in the present, more often a future question answered with an operational reflex.

An incisive question

If you knew that your attention is the most consequential resource you allocate each week, what would you place it on tomorrow?

Two

The pull of the present

Was there a moment this week where something new was being pulled back into the old way of doing things?

A pilot reframed as a delivery target; a fresh idea asked to prove itself by the old measures. It's rarely anyone's fault — it's gravity, and naming it is most of the work.

An incisive question

If you knew that the new thing only needs protecting until it can stand, what would you shield this month?

Three

The question you carry

What question deserves your best thinking before the team next meets?

Write it down. Don't answer it. Bring it with you to the next session — it may be exactly what the team needs to hear.

"If attention is the currency of leaders, time is the investment."

Mitzi Wyman

From time to time

Mitzi Wyman — Horizon Check

Before the week closes

A two-minute self-check. Rate yourself honestly on each statement. There's no pass mark — the value is in the noticing.

  • I can name where my best thinking went this week.

    Not yet Yes, clearly

  • The thinking I gave my work matched the questions it was asking.

    Not yet Yes, clearly

  • I noticed at least one place where the new was being pulled back into the old.

    Not yet Yes, clearly

  • I protected some time this week that wasn't claimed by the immediate.

    Not yet Yes, clearly

  • I'm carrying a question into next week — and I know what it is.

    Not yet Yes, clearly

There's no pass mark. The horizons are a way of seeing, and the only habit worth building is the noticing itself: where your time went, what it served, and what's quietly waiting for your attention.

I hope these few minutes have let you look at your week from a little distance. The future doesn't arrive by itself — it's built by people who give it room to be thought about.
