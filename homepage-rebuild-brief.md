# Homepage rebuild — thinking.mitziwyman.com

Brief for Claude Code. The goal is to restore the voice and philosophy the
current homepage lost, without a full redesign. Keep the existing styling,
fonts (Lora), palette (navy / teal), and the left accent bar. This is a
content and ordering change, not a visual one.

New page order, top to bottom:

1. Hero
2. Dialogic argument (the "why")
3. The two cards
4. First-person paragraph (meeting Mitzi)
5. Ikeda quote (philosophical close)
6. Footer

---

## 1. Hero

Keep the existing kicker line: **Mitzi Wyman · Time to Think**

Keep the existing H1: **Better thinking changes everything.**

**Remove** the paragraph that currently follows it ("Under relentless
pressure, clear independent thinking is often the first thing to go. This is
a space for those who want to change that…").

**Replace** it with this single opening line:

> Do you sometimes feel you barely have time to draw breath, let alone think
> about the questions that really matter?

Also **remove the Nancy Kline quote from the homepage** ("The quality of
everything we do depends on the quality of the thinking we do first…"). It
already appears on the Courses page and is doing better work there. The Ikeda
quote at the foot of this page now carries the philosophical register.

---

## 2. Dialogic argument

A new short section between the hero and the cards. This is the "why" the
current homepage is missing.

> "If attention is the currency of leaders, time is the investment."
> — Mitzi Wyman

When we rarely give each other real attention, our thinking narrows. We grow
addicted to certainty and locked into the short term, repeating cycles of
activity that only rehash the past. The meeting fills the hour and changes
nothing.

Generative attention does the opposite. When someone shows genuine interest
in where your thinking will go next, fresh thinking becomes possible — the
kind that leads somewhere new. Leaders who give that quality of attention set
the conditions for everyone around them to do the same.

## 4. First-person paragraph

Replace the current third-person bio block ("Mitzi Wyman is a lawyer by
background and a member of the Time to Think Faculty…") with this:

> Twenty years of working with senior leaders has convinced me of something
> simple: the quality of a decision depends on the quality of the thinking
> that produced it, and the quality of the thinking depends on how people
> treat each other while it's happening. I learned this the slow way, as a
> lawyer trained to be the expert in the room — the one with the answers,
> never the one listening. This site is for people who want to learn it
> faster.

Directly beneath, in smaller italic text, the credentials line so nothing is
lost institutionally:

> *Mitzi Wyman is a member of the Time to Think Faculty, trained in
> organisational psychiatry and psychology, and a facilitator with Windsor
> Leadership.*

## 5. Ikeda quote

A closing block before the footer. White text on navy (the existing dark
slate), centred, as in the original Fulcrum site. This is the philosophical
floor the page rests on.

> "Engaging in dialogue is a struggle to positively transform our own life as
> well as that of others. It is the act of breaking out of the shell of our
> lesser self, surrounding the wall of our callous ego, and creating and
> expanding positive connections with others. When we have the courage to
> meet and talk with people about our ideals, we are taking the first and
> surest step in our human revolution."
>
> — Daisaku Ikeda
